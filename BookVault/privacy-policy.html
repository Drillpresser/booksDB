GDPR — Rights for Users in the EU / EEA

If you are located in the European Union or European Economic Area, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) gives you specific rights over your personal data. This section explains how those rights apply to your use of [Your App Name].

Lawful basis for processing

We process your personal data under Article 6(1)(b) GDPR — processing is necessary to perform the contract between you and us (i.e. providing your account and the app's core functionality). We do not rely on consent or legitimate interest as our lawful basis.

International data transfers

Your data is stored on servers in the United States, which is outside the EU/EEA. The US does not have an adequacy decision from the European Commission covering all transfers. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place for any such transfer through the use of Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs) as approved by the European Commission, or by relying on the privacy commitments of our infrastructure provider (e.g. [AWS / Firebase / other] adheres to SCCs).

You may request a copy of the applicable transfer mechanism by contacting us at [your@email.com].

Your GDPR rights in full

Under the GDPR you have the right to:

  • → Access (Art. 15) — obtain a copy of your personal data
  • → Rectification (Art. 16) — correct inaccurate or incomplete data
  • → Erasure (Art. 17) — request deletion of your data ("right to be forgotten")
  • → Restriction (Art. 18) — ask us to limit how we process your data
  • → Portability (Art. 20) — receive your data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • → Object (Art. 21) — object to processing based on legitimate interests

To exercise any GDPR right, email us at [your@email.com]. We will respond within 30 days (extendable by two further months for complex requests, with notice). If you believe we have not handled your data lawfully, you have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority (e.g. the ICO in the UK, CNIL in France, or your national DPA).

Data Breach Notification

Despite our security measures, no system is completely immune to risk. In the event of a personal data breach, we will take the following steps:

  • → Contain the breach — we will immediately act to stop further unauthorised access and assess the scope of the incident.
  • → Notify regulators (GDPR) — if you are an EU/EEA user and the breach is likely to result in a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach, as required by Article 33 GDPR.
  • → Notify affected users (GDPR) — if the breach is likely to result in a high risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify you directly without undue delay (Article 34 GDPR), describing the nature of the breach, the data involved, and the steps we are taking.
  • → Notify affected users (CCPA / US state laws) — if you are a California or other US-state resident, we will notify you in the most expedient time possible and without unreasonable delay, consistent with applicable state breach notification laws.
  • → Remediate — we will take appropriate technical and organisational steps to prevent a recurrence and document the incident in an internal breach register.

Breach notifications to users will be sent to the email address associated with your account. If you believe your account has been compromised, please contact us immediately at [your@email.com].
